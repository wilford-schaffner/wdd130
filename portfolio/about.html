Wilford Schaffner

Designer & Full-time Father

Bio:

Hey! My name is Wilford Schaffner and I'm a UX/UI Designer living in Prosper, TX. My wife, my daughter and I love spending time together and going outside. I really love the opportunities technology brings into my life and how it helps me serve those I love the most. My interest in UX Design and Graphic Design actually started because of editing some videos and doing some small projects for my church, after that I really wanted to combine my passion for visual design and for serving others so I started my work in UX. I am very detail oriented so I really enjoy solving user pain points by honing in this skill. I love music, my family, and nature, and anything that can make any of those three things more enjoyable.

"How you do something is how you do everything."

I’ve loved design ever since I was a kid, whether it was nostalgic video gamed HUD’s, concept art for the new i-Phone, or beautifully made operating systems.
I think the first thing that really inspired my desire to become a designer was the advent of iOS 7.

iOS 7 was a breathtakingly stunning and simple design. It truly captivated me, and forever became my standard of a good design. I loved the flat and simple nature of the icons and graphics, juxtaposed with the glass-morphic elements of the operating system and the immersive space backgrounds. It was beautiful.

 

 

From then on I would always pay very close attention to the way things were designed, I then got heavily involved in customizing my Android phone at the time. I loved downloading custom icon-packs, then eventually even started to create some of my own.

I loved the rush I felt after successfully expressing a design idea.

 

 

Years after I served a mission for my church (The Church of Jesus-Christ of Latter-day Saints) and when COVID hit I became a social media specialist. This sparked an intense desire to have a career in design. When I came home from my mission, I completed a UX Design Course through Google, and now I am going to school to achieve a Bachelor’s Degree in Web Design and Development. I’m stoked to see what the future holds.
